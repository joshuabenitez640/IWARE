About the Project

Project I.W.A.R.E. stands for Infrastructure Watch for Assessment, Reporting, and Evaluation. This project focuses on raising awareness about the importance of checking and monitoring our community infrastructure like roads, bridges, public buildings, and drainage systems. Many people use these structures every day, but not everyone realizes that poor maintenance can lead to accidents, damage, and bigger problems in the future.

The goal of I.W.A.R.E. is to encourage people to notice issues early, report them, and help keep our surroundings safe. By spreading information and educating the public, we want everyone to take part in protecting and improving the places we live in.

"Project I.W.A.R.E. aims to build a community that cares about the safety and condition of its public spaces through awareness, reporting, and shared responsibility."

Infrastructure Issues

Infrastructure plays a huge role in our daily lives

We walk on sidewalks, ride on roads, and study or work inside buildings. When these structures are old or damaged, they can become unsafe. This is why monitoring them is important.

Some common issues include:

  • Cracks on roads and bridges
  • Clogged drainage causing floods
  • Weak or damaged building walls
  • Broken streetlights
  • Rusting metal parts in public structures

By checking these early, we can avoid accidents, delays, and costly repairs. Project I.W.A.R.E. encourages everyone to stay observant and report anything that looks unsafe.

How Reporting Makes a Difference

Sometimes people see a problem but don't know how to report it or feel like it won't matter. But reporting small issues can prevent bigger hazards.

Reporting helps because:

  • It alerts local authorities faster
  • It prevents minor damage from becoming major
  • It keeps the community safer
  • It encourages others to be responsible
  • It helps improve the overall condition of public places

Even simple actions like taking a photo, telling barangay officials, or sending a short message can already make an impact.
